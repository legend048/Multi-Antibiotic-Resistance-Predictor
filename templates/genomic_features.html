What Are Genomic Features?

Genomic features are numeric signals extracted from bacterial genome data. In this project, the model uses 40 features (F1-F40) to represent mutation markers, resistance gene signatures, and summary proportions linked to antibiotic response.

F1-F15 Binary Signals

These are usually presence or absence indicators. Example: a known mutation or resistance gene is detected (1) or not detected (0).

F16-F30 Count-like Burden

How the AI Upload Fits In

When you upload a medical or genomics note, the LLM converts unstructured text into these F1-F40 values. The predictor then uses the normalized values to estimate susceptibility for Ciprofloxacin, Ceftriaxone, and Amoxicillin.

Important: this is a decision-support workflow for research and demo use. Real clinical decisions must still be confirmed by certified AST laboratory testing.
